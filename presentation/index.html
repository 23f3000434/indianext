Why SaveWaves

Explainability Isn't a Feature.
It's the Foundation.

Any model can output "malicious" or "safe." That's not useful. A security tool that can't explain itself is a tool nobody trusts.
WHAT was flagged — Suspicious elements are highlighted directly in the input, with visual evidence so users see exactly what triggered the alert
WHY it was flagged — Each alert comes with specific threat indicators and detailed reasoning, not just a generic "spam detected" label
HOW CONFIDENT the system is — A probability score with reasoning, so even on a false positive, users can make an informed decision instead of blindly trusting or ignoring
WHAT TO DO next — Context-aware, actionable recommendations tailored to the threat — not generic "be careful" warnings, but real steps: block, report, quarantine
